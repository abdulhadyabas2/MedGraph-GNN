MedGraph-GNN: Integrating Curated Knowledge Graphs with Large Language Models to Reduce Clinical Hallucinations

1 Artificial Intelligence and Innovation Centre, University of Kurdistan Hewler, Erbil, Iraq

2 CHIRP, Child Health Research Centre, The University of Queensland, Brisbane, Australia

3 Department of Computer Engineering, University of Kurdistan, Sanandaj, Iran

Abstract

Hallucinations in medical Large Language Models (LLMs) pose critical patient safety risks, manifesting as both fabricated factual content and invalid reasoning chains. We present Medical Graph-Enhanced Graph Neural Network (MedGraph-GNN), a novel architecture that deeply integrates Attributed Relational Graphs (ARGs) with Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) to systematically suppress hallucinations in clinical question-answering systems. Our approach combines a relation-aware GNN retriever that extracts multi-hop subgraphs encoding complex clinical dependencies, a Graph-of-Thought reasoning module employing belief propagation over structured hypothesis spaces, ARG-based verification with constrained decoding to enforce factual grounding, and Circuit-based Reasoning Verification (CRV) that performs white-box analysis of model internals to detect and correct reasoning errors. Comprehensive evaluation on MedQA-USMLE and Med-HALT benchmarks (5 independent random seeds, 95% confidence intervals) demonstrates that MedGraph-GNN significantly outperforms the base model on MedQA as well as text-based retrieval-augmented generation, GraphRAG, and knowledge graph prompting baselines. Hallucination rates decrease substantially, while CRV performs well at error detection and achieves a solid correction success rate. The system provides full auditable provenance traces, essential for clinical deployment and regulatory oversight.

Key Contributions

  • 1

    Relation-Aware GNN Retrieval Architecture

    Exploits typed medical relations to extract multi-hop subgraphs encoding complex clinical dependencies, outperforming text-based and untyped graph retrieval baselines.

  • 2

    Graph-of-Thought Reasoning Module

    Organizes intermediate hypotheses in a graph structure and employs belief propagation to aggregate evidence and detect logical contradictions across multiple reasoning paths.

  • 3

    ARG-Based Verification System

    Maps generated claims to knowledge graph triples, providing both post-hoc validation and training-time reward signals that penalize unsupported content.

  • 4

    Comprehensive Empirical Evaluation

    Rigorous evaluation on MedQA-USMLE and Med-HALT benchmarks with 5 independent random seeds, 95% confidence intervals, and systematic ablation studies.

  • 5

    Circuit-Based Reasoning Verification (CRV)

    A novel white-box verification method achieving AUROC 0.87 for error detection and successfully correcting 67% of flagged reasoning steps through targeted model interventions.

Comprehensive evaluation on MedQA-USMLE and Med-HALT benchmarks (5 independent random seeds, 95% confidence intervals) demonstrates that MedGraph-GNN achieves 81.5 ± 1.2% accuracy on MedQA, significantly outperforming the base model (72.8 ± 0.9%, p < 0.001) as well as text-based retrieval-augmented generation (76.0%), GraphRAG (77.8%), and knowledge graph prompting (78.5%) baselines. Hallucination rates decrease by 73% (from 22.3 ± 2.1% to 6.1 ± 0.8%), while CRV achieves AUROC 0.87 for error detection with 67% successful correction rate. The system provides full auditable provenance traces, essential for clinical deployment and regulatory oversight. MedGraph-GNN inference latency is 7.4s per query with CRV enabled (5.6s without CRV); graph retrieval accounts for 2.1s, reasoning 1.8s, and CRV 1.8s.

Evaluation Benchmarks

Our empirical evaluation uses a comprehensive mix of medical QA benchmarks to measure exam-style reasoning, factual recall, clinical note grounding, and domain-specific inference:

MedQA-USMLE

USMLE-style multiple-choice questions covering core clinical domains; tests multi-step clinical reasoning.

Med-HALT

Targeted hallucination benchmark (False-Confidence, NOTA, Fake Questions, Memory probes) for measuring unsupported or fabricated claims.

MedMCQA & PubMedQA

Large-scale multiple-choice and research-paper-derived yes/no/factoid items for factual consistency checks.

CliCR & emrQA

Clinical reading comprehension and EHR-derived QA for document-grounded reasoning over clinical texts.

MedNLI

Clinical natural language inference dataset for testing entailment/contradiction detection in clinical statements.
